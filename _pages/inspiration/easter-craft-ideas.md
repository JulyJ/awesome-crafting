## Beginner Easter Crafts

### Decorated Easter Eggs: Classic Techniques

The heart of Easter crafting, with methods for every age and skill level.

#### Natural Dye Easter Eggs

Eco-friendly, beautiful, and kid-safe.

| Natural Material | Color Produced | Preparation | Notes |
|-----------------|----------------|-------------|-------|
| **Red cabbage** | Blue to purple | Chop, boil 30 min | Add vinegar for pink |
| **Yellow onion skins** | Orange to rust | Boil skins 30 min | Deep, rich tones |
| **Turmeric** | Golden yellow | 2-3 tbsp in water | Vibrant, consistent |
| **Beets** | Pink to red | Chop, boil 30 min | Earthy pink shades |
| **Spinach** | Light green | Boil leaves 30 min | Soft, pastel green |
| **Coffee** | Brown to tan | Brew strong coffee | Vintage look |

**Natural Dye Process:**
1. Hard boil eggs, let cool
2. Prepare dye baths (boil materials, strain)
3. Add 1 tbsp white vinegar per cup of dye
4. Submerge eggs 30 min to overnight
5. Remove, pat dry, apply vegetable oil for shine
6. Refrigerate if eating; display if decorative

**Patterns & Techniques:**
- **Rubber bands**: Wrap before dyeing for stripes
- **Leaf prints**: Press leaves against egg, wrap in pantyhose
- **Wax resist**: Draw with crayon before dyeing
- **Ombre**: Partially submerge for gradient effect

#### Decoupage Easter Eggs

Beautiful, decorative eggs using napkins or tissue paper.

| Materials | Cost | Yield | Time |
|-----------|------|-------|------|
| Plastic or blown eggs | $8-12/dozen | 12 eggs | 2-3 hours |
| Decorative napkins | $5-8 | 12 eggs | - |
| Mod Podge | $5 | 50+ eggs | - |
| Foam brush | $2 | Reusable | - |

**Step-by-Step:**
1. Prepare eggs (plastic or blow out real eggs)
2. Separate napkin layers (use only printed layer)
3. Tear or cut napkin into egg-sized pieces
4. Apply thin layer of Mod Podge to egg
5. Gently press napkin onto egg, smooth wrinkles
6. Apply another coat of Mod Podge over top
7. Let dry completely (2-4 hours)
8. Optional: Add glitter, ribbons, or embellishments

**Design Ideas:**
- **Floral**: Spring flower napkins
- **Pastel patterns**: Geometric or abstract
- **Vintage**: Ephemera, old book pages, maps
- **Themed**: Bunnies, chicks, spring scenes

**Market Potential**: $8-15 per decorated egg set (6 eggs)

### Paper Easter Basket

Perfect kids' project for egg hunts or party favors.

| Materials | Tools | Time | Age Range |
|-----------|-------|------|-----------|
| Cardstock or construction paper | Scissors, glue | 30-45 min | 5+ years |
| Template (free printable) | Pencil, ruler | - | Adult help for younger |
| Decorations (stickers, markers) | - | - | - |

**Construction Steps:**
1. Download and print basket template
2. Cut out basket shape from cardstock
3. Score fold lines (use ruler and dull knife)
4. Fold up sides, glue tabs
5. Attach handle (paper strip or pipe cleaner)
6. Decorate with markers, stickers, or stamps
7. Fill with Easter grass and treats

**Variations:**
- **Bunny basket**: Add ears to handle
- **Carrot basket**: Orange paper, green grass top
- **Egg-shaped basket**: Oval instead of traditional shape
- **Woven basket**: Cut slits, weave paper strips

**Classroom/Party Use**: 24 baskets cost ~$10-15 in materials

**Popular Sign Sayings:**

- "Some Bunny Loves You"
- "Easter Blessings"
- "He Is Risen"
- "Spring is in the Air"

**Design Process:**

**Method 1: Stencil (Beginner-Friendly)**
1. Sand wood board smooth
2. Paint base color (white, cream, pastel)
3. Let dry completely
4. Create or purchase stencil (use MyLaserTools text!)
5. Position stencil, tape in place
6. Dab paint over stencil (don't brush)
7. Remove stencil carefully
8. Add hand-painted details (flowers, eggs)
9. Seal with polyurethane

**Method 2: Vinyl Mask (Intermediate)**
1. Sand and paint base
2. Cut vinyl text/design with Cricut
3. Apply vinyl as mask
4. Paint over entire surface (contrasting color)
5. Peel vinyl to reveal design
6. Distress edges with sandpaper (farmhouse look)
7. Seal

**Finishing Touches:**
- Sawtooth hanger on back
- Twine rope for hanging
- 3D elements (mini eggs, faux flowers)
- Laser-cut layered embellishments

## Troubleshooting Common Easter Craft Problems

| Issue | Project Type | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------------|-------|----------|
| **Dye won't stick to eggs** | Egg decorating | Oily egg surface | Wipe with vinegar before dyeing |
| **Vinyl won't stick to basket** | Personalization | Textured surface | Use strong-hold vinyl, heat with hairdryer |
| **3D print layer lines visible** | 3D printing | Low resolution | Sand with 220-400 grit, prime & paint |
| **Felt ornaments look sloppy** | Sewing | Uneven stitching | Practice blanket stitch, use embroidery hoop |
| **Paint bleeds under stencil** | Wood signs | Too much paint | Dab, don't brush; seal stencil edges |
| **Paper basket falls apart** | Kids' crafts | Weak paper, poor glue | Use cardstock, white glue or hot glue |
