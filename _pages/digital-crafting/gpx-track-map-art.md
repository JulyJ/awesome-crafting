A **GPX file** is the GPS breadcrumb trail of something you did - a marathon, a century ride, a thru-hike, a sailing passage. With the free [Running Route Map preset](https://mylasertools.com/map-designer?preset=running-route-map) on MyLaserTools you can draw that track as a **bold line over a map**, so the route becomes the hero and the surrounding city or terrain becomes the proof you were there. It's the most personal map gift there is.

## Where to Get Your GPX File

| Source | How |
|---|---|
| Strava | Activity menu -> Export GPX |
| Garmin Connect | Activity -> gear icon -> Export to GPX |
| COROS / Suunto / Apple Health | Export Original / Export GPX |
| Race timing sites | Often offer a downloadable GPX of the course |

## Step 1: Import Your Track

Open the [Running Route Map preset](https://mylasertools.com/map-designer?preset=running-route-map). It loads with a sample loop in Chicago. Use **Add layer -> Route** and upload your `.gpx`, then delete the sample. Re-search the location to your area and frame the route:

- Frame the **route**, not the whole city: zoom until the track fills the middle 60-70% of the frame.
- **Point-to-point** routes (a marathon, a thru-hike) read best corner to corner; loops look great centred.
- Leave one **quiet corner** for a caption: event name, date, distance, finishing time.

## Step 2: Style the Route and Base

| Layer | Styling |
|---|---|
| **Route** | Bold crimson line; thicken to width 7 for long or engraved versions. Keep it the boldest mark |
| **Base map** | Quiet off-white land, soft water, muted roads so the route pops |
| **Backplate** | Deep navy; shift toward black for a sportier look |

Want terrain instead of streets? Drop the track over a [topographic base](https://mylasertools.com/map-designer?preset=topography) for a backcountry hike or ski route.

## Step 3: Export for Your Method

| Method | Export |
|---|---|
| **Framed print** | High-res PNG (4096 px), Background ON |
| **Laser engrave** | Combined SVG; route becomes the deepest tone |
| **Layered build** | Base-map SVG (engrave) + Route SVG (cut/inlay) + backplate |

## Build It

**Framed print:** Print at 250-300 mm on matte paper, add the event details as a caption, and frame with a mat board.

**Laser engrave on wood or slate:** Engrave the base lightly and the route bold. **Paint-fill the engraved route** with a fine crimson paint pen before de-masking for a colour pop, then finish with oil.

**Layered build:** Cut a navy backplate, engrave the base map on an off-white square glued centred, and cut the route from crimson acrylic glued on top following the engraved line beneath.

## Make It Yours

- **Finisher's gift:** route plus "BMO Vancouver Marathon - 42.2 km - 3:42:11" engraved under the map.
- **Series wall:** one plaque per race, the route colour shifting down a hallway.
- **Multi-activity piece:** upload several GPX files as separate route layers in different colours - a whole training block on one map.
- **Sailing or paddling passage:** draw the track over a coastal or bathymetry base for a nautical keepsake.
